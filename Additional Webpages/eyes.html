EYES MAKEUP


Smokey Eyes

Smokey eyes are a timeless makeup technique that exudes allure, mystery, and sophistication. This iconic eye makeup style is characterized by a seamless gradient of dark eyeshadow, creating a sultry, smokey effect around the eyes. Whether you're aiming for a dramatic evening look or a subtle daytime glam, mastering the art of smokey eyes can elevate your makeup game to new heights. To achieve the perfect smokey eyes, start by prepping your eyelids with a primer to create a smooth canvas and ensure longevity. Then, select your eyeshadow shades. While traditional smokey eyes are typically done with shades of black or grey, you can experiment with various colors like deep browns, purples, blues, or even greens to complement your eye color and outfit. Begin by applying a neutral base eyeshadow all over your eyelids to create a uniform base. Next, take a darker shade and apply it to the outer corner of your eyes, blending it into the crease using a fluffy eyeshadow brush. Make sure to blend seamlessly to avoid harsh lines. Once you've established the base of your smokey eye, deepen the intensity by layering darker eyeshadow shades gradually. Concentrate the darkest shade closest to the lash line and blend upwards towards the crease, building depth and dimension.

Shimmery Eyes

Shimmery eyes bring a captivating sparkle to your gaze, adding dimension and allure to any makeup look. Whether you're aiming for a subtle shimmer for daytime glam or a bold, metallic statement for a night out, mastering the art of shimmery eye makeup can instantly elevate your beauty game.

Here's how to achieve stunning shimmery eyes:

  • Prep the Eyelids
  • Select Your Shimmery Eyeshadows
  • Apply a Base Eyeshadow
  • Add Shimmer to the Lid
  • Blend the Crease
  • Highlight the Brow Bone
  • Illuminate the Inner Corner
  • Optional: Define with Eyeliner and Mascara
  • Set Your Look

Gradient makeup

Gradient eye makeup is a versatile and visually stunning technique that involves blending multiple eyeshadow colors seamlessly to create a gradient effect across the eyelids. This makeup style can range from subtle transitions of similar shades to bold contrasts of complementary colors, allowing for endless creativity and expression.

Here's how to achieve a captivating gradient eye makeup look:

  • Prep and Prime
  • Select Your Eyeshadow Colors
  • Arrange Your Shades
  • Apply the Lightest Shade
  • Blend the Transition
  • Layer and Blend
  • Deepen the Outer Corner
  • Blend and Soften Edges
  • Highlight the Brow Bone
  • Optional: Define with Eyeliner and Mascara
  • Set Your Look
